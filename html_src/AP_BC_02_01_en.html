In-Preson Entry Inspection

In-Preson Entry Inspection Information

  • The immigration screening is the process of being approved at the border and distinguishes between nationals and foreigners.
  • Foreigners (except for registered foreigners) must fill out the entry form, and those who are eligible to register must visit the Immigrations Bureau within 90 days of entry to register.
  • Chinese group tourists with group visas do not need to fill out the entry form (exception: youth travelling on school field trip)
  • There has been an increase of foreigners attempting to enter by forging a Korean passport. Please cooperate with the immigration officer’s questions and extensive screening even if it might feel like an inconvenience.

Exemption from filling out the Entry Reporting Form

  • Korean nationals and foreigners who plan to reside long-term (over 90 days) and have registered already with the Immigration Bureau do not need to fill out the entry form.

Screening process

  1. step 01
    Foreigners who did not fill out the entry form must fill out the entry form before the immigration screening.

    (Korean nationals and registered foreigners exempt)

  2. step 02
    Check that you are in the correct line as the waiting areas for nationals and foreigners are separate.

    Nationals follow the blue line, foreigners follow the red line.

  3. step 03
    Step up to the desk when the gate opens.
  4. step 04
    Nationals present the passport and the foreigners present the passport and entry form to the immigration officer. Once the screening is over, proceed through the screening area.

    Foreigners over 17 years of age must scan their fingerprints and have their face photographed.
