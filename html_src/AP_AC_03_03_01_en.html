Quarantine Inspection Guidelines

Quarantine Declaration

The Quarantine Inspection Station issues inspections and certificates for foreign travelers, animals, and plants. Depending on the destination country, you might be required to submit an inspection certificate, so please check ahead of time.

You must complete the quarantine inspection related procedure before boarding procedure to avoid any delays in boarding.

Yellow Fever
  • Africa : Angola, People's Republic of Benin, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Congo, Cote d’ivoire, Gabon, Ghana, Guinea Bissau, Liberia, Mali, Niger, Rwanda, São Tomé and Príncipe, Sierra Leon, Togo, Equatorial Guinea, Ethiopia, Uganda, Tanzania, Gambia, Guinea, Kenya, Mauritania, Nigeria, Senegal, Somalia, Sudan
  • America : French Guiana, Argentina, Ecuador, Venezuela, Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Guiana, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, Suriname, Trinidad and Tobago

Africa and Central and South America pose high risk for yellow fever contagion. Those traveling to these areas must receive the yellow fever vaccination at least 10 days before departure and carry the Yellow Card as proof..

The vaccination is valid for a lifetime starting 10 days after receiving the vaccination, and in the case of missing Yellow Card, it is possible to reissue the Yellow Card.

Cholera
(Not mandatory)
  • Africa, Central and South America, Southeast Asia

Oral cholera vaccine taken twice (1-6 weeks between each dose) will protect 85-90％ and is recommended for travelers or long-term visitors to high-risk areas.

For more information on yellow fever, please visit the Korean Center for Disease Control website. Korean Center for Disease Control website
