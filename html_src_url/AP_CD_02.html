Food&Lifestyle

  • Songdo (G-Tower + NC Cube Canal Walk) 2-HrTour
    COURSE
    Incheon International Airport → Songdo G-Tower →
    Songdo NC Cube Canal Walk → Incheon International Airport
    Schedule
    Terminal 1 14:00~16:00
    Terminal 2 16:00~18:00

    G-Tower Located at Central Park in Songdo International City, G-Tower is one of the most popular landmarks of Incheon. The observatory on the 33rd floor boasts a magnificent 360 panoramic view of Songdo International City. The Sky Garden located on the 29th floor also offers an open 180-degree view of Songdo Central Park, Incheon Bridge and the west coast of the city of Incheon.

    Songdo NC Cube Canal Walk which opened in August 2013 is Songdo’s premium outlet that has colorful, unique cafes, brand stores and venues where cultural events and performances are held. The mall is divided into four blocks, to correspond with the four seasons and the canal-like artificial waterway that runs through those blocks create a wonderfully romantic atmosphere.

According to the records, 52kg of gold was mined from 1955 to 1972. The mine was closed down in 1972 but it is estimated that the cave still has a sizable amount of gold deposits.

Temple

  • Jeondeungsa Temple Deokjinjin 4-HrTour
    COURSE
    Incheon International Airport → Jeondeungsa Temple →
    Incheon International Airport
    Schedule
    Terminal 1&2 8:00~12:00

    Jeondeungsa Temple is considered the oldest temple in Korea and is known to have been built in the late 4th century, around when Buddhism was first introduced to Korea. In the 13th century, during the reign of King Wonjong of Goryeo Dynasty, the temple was rebuilt and played a significant role in the revival of Buddhism. It is a prestigious temple that holds a beautiful Daewoongjeon (Main Buddha Hall) designated as Treasure No. 178 of Korea as well as various valuable relics from Goryeo and Joseon Dynasty periods.

    Deokjinjin was a strategic point of the outer castle wall used for defending the Ganghwa Straits during the Goryeo Dynasty (918-1392). It was the strongest artillery base in the Ganghwa Straits and a historical site where fierce battles during Byeonginyangyo (the French invasion in 1866) and Shinmiyangyo (the American invasion in 1871) took place. The battlements and gatehouse of Deokjinjin that had all been destroyed during Sinmiyangyo were restored later in 1970. Treasure No. 178 of Korea as well as various valuable relics from Goryeo and Joseon Dynasty periods.

  • Cheonggyecheon Stream Jogyesa Temple 4-HrTour
    COURSE
    Incheon International Airport → Cheonggyecheon Stream →
    Jogyesa Temple → Incheon International Airport
    Schedule
    Terminal 1&2 14:00~18:00

    Cheonggyecheon Stream Cheonggyecheon Streamrunning between Seoul’s Jongno-gu and Jung-gu holds not only the 600-year history of Seoul but the lives and memories of Korean people who made their livelihoods around the stream. The stream was once a small watercourse hidden by an overpass but a massive urban renewal project transformed the stream and now it is one of the most popular and loved places in Seoul. Treasure No. 178 of Korea as well as various valuable relics from Goryeo and Joseon Dynasty periods.

    Jogyesa Temple Jogyesa is the chief temple of the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism and the center of Korea’s modern Buddhist movement. The grand Daewoongjeon (Main Temple) boasts splendid ornaments and golden Buddhastatues. A 500-year-old white pine tree designated as Natural Monument No.9 in Korea stands peacefully in front of Daewoongjeon, adding a touch of natural beauty to Jogyesa Temple. The temple also holds annual lantern festival in celebration of Buddhist's birthday.
